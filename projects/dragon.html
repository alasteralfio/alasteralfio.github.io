Dragon Playground

Modelled in Blender.

Overview

Made in Blender, this model features a high-quality, exact to scale dragon playground found in the deepest parts of our childhood in Singapore.

Purpose

With very little prior experience in Blender, I wanted to work on this project to improve my skills in the software. I chose the dragon playground as it is a very iconic part of Singapore's history, and I wanted to recreate it in a 3D model. Moreover, a quick search online showed that there were very few 3D models of the playground, and none that were free, so I decided to make one myself.

Process

This began with a lot of research on the playground, as I wanted to make sure that the model was as accurate as possible. I spent a full day looking for references, and thankfully, I was able to find a scale blueprint of the full playground, which became my foundation for everything.

I then started modelling the playground, working on just the front face at first. This in itself took a ton of time, as with my inexperience in blender, my meshes looked horrible.

After redoing it 4 times or so, I was finally satisfied with my work. I then went on to work on the sides and back, which were much easier to do, as I had already gotten the hang of the software, and all I had to do was copy and paste the same elements. You can find the file here.
