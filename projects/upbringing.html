Upbringing

A thrilling horror puzzle journey.
Built with Unity, designed in Photoshop.

Overview

Upbringing is a VR Game built in Unity, with compatibility for the HTC Vive platform in mind. The game uses Unity's VR Interaction Toolkit and is coded in C#, and all 2D assets were custom made in Photoshop and GIMP.

Purpose

Originally, I planned to build this game for a school assignment. As time went on through the development process, I slowly fell in love with the game and decided to put in my all.

This game takes place in a haunted toy house, a representation of a twisted past and a traumatic childhood. The player must navigate these horrors, avoiding Mother, the antagonist, and solving puzzles to escape.

Process

Firstly, I started out with working on the lighting in my scene. I knew I wanted a darker atmosphere, with lighting that feels intentional. Through the help of many youtube videos, I finally got the lighting I wanted.

Afterwards, I began sketching the room layouts, deciding the puzzles, and the overall flow of the game. I wanted to make sure the player felt a sense of dread and urgency as they navigated the house.

Finally, I began working on the assets. All of the 2D assets, such as the walls, floors, manequeins, and other smaller props were created entirely by me in Photoshop. The manequeins' poses were sketched out beforehand, albeit very roughly, and the symbols on them were freestyled on the spot, with reference to Minecraft's iconic "Enchantment Table" symbols.

The 3D assets, such as the doors, the key, and the flashlight, were all downloaded from the Unity Asset Store, and consequently modified to fit the game's aesthetic.

Once all the game's visuals were done, it was time to begin the coding. This process took the most time by far, as I had to code the AI for the chasing manequeins, the randomization for the poses and the symbols, and the final puzzle, Simon Says. The Simon Says puzzle took the longest to code, as I had to make sure the randomization actually worked, and the sequence was saved to a variable to be checked against the player's input.

Finally, I added the finishing touches like sound, particle effects, and some post processing on the lighting.
